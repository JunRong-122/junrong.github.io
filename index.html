ABOUT

I am Jun-Rong Huang, a graduate student in the Master of Computational Social Science program at the University of California, San Diego. My research focuses on aging and the well-being of older adults, with a methodological emphasis on applying computational approaches to social science questions.

My master's thesis, provisionally titled A Study on Factors Influencing the Sense of Powerlessness among Older Adults: The Moderating and Mediating Effects of Productive Aging, examines how social and productive engagement shape older adults’ psychological well-being.

Recently, I have been working with the Homelessness Hub at UC San Diego on a capstone project titled Geography and Sentiment in Homeless Encampment Reporting, where we analyze spatial patterns and public sentiment related to homelessness reporting.
