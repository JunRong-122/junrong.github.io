ABOUT

I am a computational social scientist with training in social work, studying aging, social engagement, and the well-being of older adults. I am currently completing a Master of Science in Computational Social Science at the University of California, San Diego. Prior to coming to the United States, I earned a master’s degree in Social Work from National Taiwan University.

My research focuses on applying computational methods to social work and social welfare research. In particular, I am interested in aging, homelessness, and other social issues that affect vulnerable populations. Through this work, I seek to use data science approaches to better understand social problems and inform policy and practice.

My master’s thesis at National Taiwan University examined how productive engagement moderates and mediates the relationship between social factors and perceived powerlessness among older adults.

At UC San Diego, I am a researcher with the Homelessness Hub, where I analyze spatial patterns and public discourse surrounding homeless encampment reporting using text mining methods.
