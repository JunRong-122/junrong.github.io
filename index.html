ABOUT

I am a computational social scientist specializing in aging, social engagement, and the well-being of older adults. I am currently completing my Master of Science in Computational Social Science at the University of California, San Diego.

My research examines how social and structural contexts shape psychological well-being in later life, with a methodological emphasis on longitudinal analysis and computational approaches. My master’s thesis, A Study on Factors Influencing the Sense of Powerlessness among Older Adults: The Moderating and Mediating Effects of Productive Aging, investigates how productive engagement moderates and mediates the relationship between social factors and perceived powerlessness.

I am also a researcher with the Homelessness Hub at UC San Diego, where I analyze spatial patterns and public discourse surrounding homeless encampment reporting. Using text mining and sentiment analysis, I examine how policy environments and governance practices may shape public attitudes toward homelessness.
